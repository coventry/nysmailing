Tell New Yorkers they're about to be disenfranchised!

You can use this form to generate fliers you can print out and mail to NYS residents who are not registered as Democrat, to inform them of the looming Oct 9 deadline after which many NYS voters will be unable to participate in the 2016 NYS Democratic Presidential Primary.

There is a phone banking effort underway to persuade as many NYS voters as possible to register by October 9. However, the list of voters with public snail-mail addresses is about 100 times larger than the phone-bank list, according to the numbers I've heard. Also, although it's more expensive, mailing the voter registration form to people is considerably less effort per contact, and probably more likely to encourage them to take action as it puts the necessary materials right at their finger tips.

To use these fliers, print them out with the address sheets on one side and the first page of the registration form on the other. Put stamps on the two "postage" boxes, and fold them into quarters so that the recipient's address is exposed and the board of elections address is hidden. That means folding vertically first, then horizontally. Seal the letter with a small piece of sticky tape, and you're ready to save a voter from disenfranchisement!
